The Nihilist Penguin

A short note on how a scene from Werner Herzog's Encounters at the End of the World became an online symbol of exhaustion, detachment, and the desire to walk away from modern noise.

The example shows how archival media can acquire a second life online: recommendation systems remove a scene from its original context, while repeated editing and circulation turn it into shared emotional shorthand.

Economics and markets

Markets, valuation, and long-term investing

I am interested in how economic cycles, business fundamentals, and investor behaviour are reflected in market prices. I use simple data analysis and backtesting to examine long-term index investing, systematic accumulation, drawdowns, and the limits of discretionary market timing.

What I explore

My notes compare fixed monthly investing with phased or correction-based accumulation, while treating the fixed plan as the base and recognising that historical outperformance does not guarantee a repeatable timing advantage.

I also study how valuation changes with the type of business: established companies can often be examined using earnings, price-to-earnings ratios, dividend yield, return on equity, and free-cash-flow yield, whereas younger growth companies require more emphasis on future profitability and cash-flow potential.

These are personal, educational analyses—not investment advice.
