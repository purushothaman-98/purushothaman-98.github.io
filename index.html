About

Dedicated to understanding materials from first principles

I am a PhD Researcher at the Computational Materials Modeling (COMMIT) Lab at the University of Antwerp, Belgium. My research centers on computational modeling of electronic structure and material properties using density functional theory. My work investigates the interplay between atomic structure and macroscopic material behavior, with particular emphasis on understanding how microscopic phenomena can be engineered to produce desired optical and electronic responses.

My academic training spans computational materials science and strongly correlated systems. I work primarily with DFT methods to investigate electronic properties, optical behavior, and transport phenomena in novel materials systems. Through systematic computational studies combined with theoretical analysis, I develop strategies for rational material design and property optimization.

Research Focus

  • Electronic Structure Advanced density functional theory calculations to map electronic structure, band gaps, and charge density distributions in novel materials. Understanding the fundamental electronic properties that govern material behavior.
  • 2D Materials Investigation of layered systems with emphasis on optical response, strain effects, and transition-metal doping strategies. Exploring how reduced dimensionality modifies electronic and photonic behavior.
  • Band Engineering Strategic manipulation of electronic band structures through doping, strain, and heterostructure design. Tuning optical and transport properties for applications in photoelectrochemistry and optoelectronics.
  • Optical Properties Investigation of light-matter interactions through calculated absorption spectra, optical transitions, and excitonic effects. Connecting microscopic electronic structure to macroscopic optical behavior.
