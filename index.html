About

Dedicated to understanding materials from first principles

I am a PhD Researcher at the Computational Materials Modeling (COMMIT) Lab at the University of Antwerp, Belgium. My research centers on computational modeling of electronic structure and material properties using density functional theory.

My academic training spans computational materials science and strongly correlated systems. I work primarily with DFT methods to investigate electronic properties, optical behavior, and transport phenomena in novel materials systems.

Computational Methods

VASP, GGA-PBE, DFT+U, band structure analysis, optical properties

Specialization

2D materials, oxide engineering, transition-metal systems, electronic property tuning

Programming & Tools

Python, MATLAB, Materials Studio, USPEX, data visualization

P.M

Research Focus

Electronic Structure

Advanced density functional theory calculations to map electronic structure, band gaps, and charge density distributions in novel materials.

2D Materials

Investigation of layered systems with emphasis on optical response, strain effects, and transition-metal doping strategies.

Band Engineering

Systematic computational studies of d-orbital engineering in oxides to tune electronic and optical properties.

Property Optimization

Combining high-throughput computations with targeted design to optimize material properties for applications.
