About Me

I'm a PhD researcher at the University of Antwerp, specializing in computational materials science and engineering. My work focuses on understanding and designing novel materials at the atomic scale using first-principles calculations and density functional theory (DFT).

My research interests span 2D materials, band engineering, electronic structure calculations, and machine learning applications in materials discovery. I'm passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical calculations and experimental validation.

When I'm not immersed in simulations, I enjoy writing about materials science trends and communicating complex scientific concepts to diverse audiences.

Research Areas
