About

I am a physicist and computational materials science researcher. As a Junior Research Fellow at the Materials Modeling and Simulation Laboratory, IIITDM Kancheepuram, I contributed to the DST–SERB-funded project “Cost-effective and High-performance Transparent Conductors.”

My work spans density functional theory, spin–orbit-coupled electronic structure, quasiparticle corrections, excitonic effects, and transport analysis. I am particularly interested in turning rigorous electronic-structure calculations into physical insight that can guide experiments and materials design.

Research directions

01

Electronic structure

Band topology, density of states, orbital character, spin–orbit coupling, defects, doping, and strain-dependent band engineering.

02

Excited-state properties

GW quasiparticle corrections and Bethe–Salpeter calculations of optical spectra, excitons, binding energies, and momentum-space character.

03

Functional materials

First-principles design of transparent conductors, layered semiconductors, and 2D heterostructures for electronic and optoelectronic applications.

Selected research projects

DFT → GW → BSE

Many-body properties of Bi₂O₂Se

Investigating dimensionality, strain, quasiparticle renormalization, optical response, and excitonic states in bulk and monolayer Bi₂O₂Se using Quantum ESPRESSO, Yambo, and Python.

  • Spin–orbit-coupled direct and indirect gaps
  • GW corrections and BSE optical spectra
  • Exciton binding energies and k-space composition

2D heterostructures

Interlayer excitons in WSe₂/WS₂

Comparing stacking-dependent electronic and optical behavior in AA and AB heterobilayers, including layer character and interlayer versus intralayer excitons.

  • Layer-resolved band alignment
  • Exciton energies and oscillator strengths
  • Comparison with experimental photoluminescence

Transparent conductors

Strain and transition-metal doping in SrVO₃

Studying how structural distortion and chemical substitution tune optoelectronic properties relevant to transparent conducting applications.

  • DFT and DFT+U electronic structure
  • Strain- and doping-dependent response
  • Published in the Journal of Electronic Materials
