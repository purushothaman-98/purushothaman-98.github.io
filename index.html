Welcome

I am a physicist working in computational materials science. My research uses first-principles and many-body calculations to study the electronic, optical, and transport properties of functional materials.

I have worked as a Junior Research Fellow at the Materials Modeling and Simulation Laboratory, IIITDM Kancheepuram, on the DST–SERB-funded project “Cost-effective and High-performance Transparent Conductors.”

My current interests include density functional theory, GW and Bethe–Salpeter calculations, two-dimensional materials, defects, strain, doping, and transparent conductors.

I am interested in PhD opportunities in computational materials science and electronic-structure theory.

Research at a glance

  • Electronic-structure calculations using DFT and DFT+U
  • Quasiparticle and optical properties using GW–BSE
  • Spin–orbit coupling, strain, defects, and chemical substitution
  • Scientific programming, data analysis, and high-performance computing
