About

From atomic structure to measurable properties

My background is in physics and computational materials science. I use quantum-mechanical simulations to study how structure, dimensionality, strain, defects, and chemical substitution influence material properties.

Before beginning my PhD, I worked as a Junior Research Fellow at the Materials Modeling and Simulation Laboratory, IIITDM Kancheepuram, on the DST–SERB-funded project “Cost-effective and High-performance Transparent Conductors.”
