About

From atomic structure to measurable properties

My work follows a connected DFT–GW–BSE workflow. Density Functional Theory establishes the relaxed structure, charge density, band dispersion, density of states, and orbital character. The GW approximation refines quasiparticle energies through screened many-body interactions, while the Bethe–Salpeter Equation introduces electron–hole attraction to describe optical absorption, exciton energies, oscillator strengths, and the momentum-space origin of spectral features.

Before beginning my PhD, I was a Junior Research Fellow at the Materials Modeling and Simulation Laboratory, IIITDM Kancheepuram, working on the DST–SERB project “Cost-effective and High-performance Transparent Conductors.”
