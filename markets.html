Notebook frameworks

How I organise the questions

Choosing and monitoring an index fund

The benchmark comes first: a NIFTY 50 fund should be evaluated against the appropriate total-return index, not merely against another fund's recent return. I then examine expense ratio, tracking difference, tracking error, portfolio disclosure, liquidity, and whether the plan is direct or regular.

Tracking difference shows the return actually lost relative to the benchmark; tracking error shows how variable that gap has been. A low advertised fee is useful only when the fund also follows its benchmark consistently.

Fixed SIP, corrections, and drawdowns

A fixed Systematic Investment Plan is the base case because it keeps participation independent of forecasts. A correction-based contribution can be tested as a separate rule only when the comparison uses identical total cash, realistic dates, and cash reserved in advance.

I compare invested capital, current value, XIRR, drawdown, recovery time, and rolling-period outcomes. A strategy winning in one realised path does not prove that discretionary timing is repeatable.

Valuing established and growth companies

For established companies with meaningful current profits, I focus on earnings quality, price-to-earnings ratio, dividend yield, return on equity, free cash flow, debt, and the firm's own valuation history.

For younger growth businesses, today's P/E can be misleading. The central questions become addressable market, revenue durability, unit economics, operating leverage, dilution, and the mature free cash flow the business might produce under explicit scenarios.

Reading equities, gold, rates, and the dollar together

Indian equity returns reflect domestic earnings and liquidity but are also exposed to global rates and foreign flows. Indian gold combines the international gold price with USD/INR, import policy, tax, and local market spreads. Higher U.S. rates and a stronger dollar can tighten financial conditions, but these relationships are regime-dependent rather than fixed trading signals.
