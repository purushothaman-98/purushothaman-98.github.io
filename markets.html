Reading the cycle

How the four series connect

NIFTY and systematic investing

Long-term investor discussions usually treat a regular investment plan as the base. Drawdowns can be studied as part of the cycle, but a backtest of successful timing is not proof that the same timing rule will work again.

Gold in Indian rupees

Indian gold reflects both the international gold price and the rupee–dollar exchange rate. It is often discussed as a diversifier or store of value, rather than a direct substitute for productive equity assets.

Federal Reserve rates

Higher policy rates raise the return available on cash and bonds and can tighten financial conditions. Lower rates can support risk assets and non-yielding gold, but inflation, growth, and expectations can change the relationship.

The dollar index

A stronger dollar often creates pressure for dollar-priced assets and global liquidity, while a weaker dollar can support gold. These are tendencies—not fixed trading rules—and can break during stress regimes.
