Research

Two-dimensional materials

Electronic and excitonic properties of TMDs and other 2D materials

I study how dimensionality, stacking, strain, and spin–orbit coupling affect the electronic and optical properties of layered materials. The work combines density functional theory with GW quasiparticle corrections and the Bethe–Salpeter equation.

  • Direct and indirect electronic transitions
  • Layer-resolved band alignment in heterostructures
  • Optical spectra, exciton energies, and oscillator strengths
  • Exciton binding and momentum-space composition
Functional materials

Transparent conductors

I investigate the influence of strain and chemical substitution on electronic and optical properties relevant to transparent conducting applications.

  • DFT and DFT+U electronic structure
  • Strain- and doping-dependent properties
  • Connection between calculated properties and materials design
