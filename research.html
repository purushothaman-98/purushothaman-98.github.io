Research

Electronic and optical properties of materials

My work combines first-principles electronic-structure calculations, many-body perturbation theory, and scientific computing.

2D materials

TMDs and other low-dimensional materials

I investigate how dimensionality, stacking, strain, and spin–orbit coupling influence the electronic and optical response of layered materials and heterostructures.

  • Direct and indirect electronic transitions
  • Layer-resolved band alignment
  • GW quasiparticle corrections and BSE optical spectra
  • Exciton energies, oscillator strengths, and momentum-space composition
Functional materials

Transparent conducting materials

I study how strain and chemical substitution modify electronic and optical properties relevant to transparent conducting applications.

  • DFT and DFT+U electronic structures
  • Strain- and doping-dependent behaviour
  • Transport and optoelectronic property analysis
